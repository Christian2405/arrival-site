# Plumbing Code Reference — Field Quick Reference

## Water Supply Pipe Sizing

### Minimum Pipe Sizes
| Fixture | Minimum Supply | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Lavatory (bathroom sink) | 3/8 inch | Hot and cold |
| Kitchen sink | 1/2 inch | Hot and cold |
| Bathtub/shower | 1/2 inch | Hot and cold |
| Toilet (tank type) | 3/8 inch | Cold only |
| Washing machine | 1/2 inch | Hot and cold |
| Dishwasher | 3/8 inch | Hot only |
| Hose bib | 1/2 inch | Cold, with vacuum breaker |
| Water heater | 3/4 inch | Both inlet and outlet |

### Main Supply Sizing
- Single-family up to 3 bathrooms: 3/4 inch main typically sufficient
- 3-5 bathrooms: 1 inch main recommended
- Over 5 bathrooms or multi-story: engineered sizing required
- Street to meter: per utility requirements
- Meter to house: minimum 3/4 inch residential

### Pressure Requirements
- Minimum: 40 PSI at highest fixture
- Maximum: 80 PSI (pressure reducing valve required above 80 PSI)
- Recommended residential: 50-70 PSI

## Drain, Waste, and Vent (DWV) Pipe Sizing

### Minimum Drain Sizes
| Fixture | Minimum Drain | Minimum Trap |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Lavatory | 1-1/4 inch | 1-1/4 inch |
| Kitchen sink | 1-1/2 inch | 1-1/2 inch |
| Bathtub | 1-1/2 inch | 1-1/2 inch |
| Shower | 2 inch | 2 inch |
| Toilet | 3 inch (4 inch preferred) | Built-in trap |
| Washing machine | 2 inch standpipe | 2 inch |
| Floor drain | 2 inch | 2 inch |

### Building Drain/Sewer
- Minimum 3 inch for residential building drain (where only 1-2 toilets connect)
- 4 inch preferred for building drain/sewer
- Minimum 4 inch for building sewer outside foundation

### Drain Slope Requirements
- **1/4 inch per foot** for pipes 3 inches and smaller
- **1/8 inch per foot** for pipes 4 inches and larger
- Maximum slope: avoid exceeding 1/2 inch per foot (solids separation risk)

## Venting Requirements

### General Vent Rules
- Every trap must be vented
- Vent must connect above the flood level rim of the highest fixture served
- Vent pipe through roof: extend minimum 6 inches above roof (12 inches in snow areas)
- Vent termination: minimum 10 feet from any operable window, door, or air intake if within 3 feet of the opening
- Minimum vent size: 1-1/4 inch (individual vent), match drain size in many codes

### Trap Arm Length (Maximum Distance from Trap to Vent)
| Trap Size | Max Trap Arm Length |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 1-1/4 inch | 5 feet (UPC) / 5 feet (IPC) |
| 1-1/2 inch | 6 feet (UPC) / 6 feet (IPC) |
| 2 inch | 8 feet (UPC) / 8 feet (IPC) |
| 3 inch | 12 feet (UPC) / 12 feet (IPC) |
| 4 inch | 16 feet (UPC) / 16 feet (IPC) |

### Types of Venting
- **Individual vent:** One vent for one fixture — most straightforward
- **Common vent:** Single vent serving two fixtures on the same level (back-to-back)
- **Wet vent:** Drain pipe from one fixture serves as vent for another (has specific rules)
- **Air admittance valve (AAV):** Mechanical vent — allowed in some jurisdictions, not all. Cannot be used for entire building vent. UPC does NOT allow AAVs — IPC does.
- **Island vent (loop vent):** For kitchen islands where conventional venting is impossible

## Water Heater Requirements

### Temperature and Pressure Relief (T&P) Valve
- Required on ALL water heaters — no exceptions
- Must discharge to within 6 inches of floor or to an approved indirect waste receptor
- Discharge pipe: same size as T&P valve outlet (usually 3/4 inch)
- Discharge pipe must pitch downward, no traps, no valves, no threads on end
- Cannot discharge where it could cause personal injury or property damage
- Cannot be connected to building drainage system in most jurisdictions

### Expansion Tank
- Required when a backflow preventer or check valve is installed on the cold water supply
- This creates a "closed system" — thermal expansion has nowhere to go
- Expansion tank absorbs the pressure spike when water heats and expands

### Seismic Strapping
- Required in seismic zones (California, Pacific Northwest, other high-risk areas)
- Two straps: upper third and lower third of water heater
- Must be secured to wall studs or masonry

### Clearances
- Gas water heater: clearance from combustible materials per manufacturer specs (typically 1 inch sides, 6 inches front)
- Top clearance to combustibles: typically 12 inches minimum
- Combustion air: required for gas water heaters in confined spaces (50 cubic feet per 1000 BTU/hour)

### Gas Connection
- Sediment trap (drip leg): required at gas connection — minimum 3 inches long
- Gas shutoff valve: required within 6 feet of appliance, in same room, accessible
- Flex connector: maximum 3 feet (6 feet in some jurisdictions)

## Fixture Installation Requirements

### Toilet Installation
- Minimum 15 inches from center of toilet to side wall or obstruction
- Minimum 15 inches from center to adjacent fixture
- Minimum 21 inches clear in front of toilet (24 inches recommended)
- Flange must be level, flush with or above finished floor
- Wax ring or approved gasket at flange connection

### Shower/Tub
- Anti-scald mixing valve required (thermostatic or pressure-balanced)
- Maximum water temperature: 120°F at point of use (some jurisdictions 110°F for showers)
- Shower pan: waterproof membrane, sloped to drain, tested for leaks before tile
- Shower drain: 2 inch minimum
- Access panel required behind tub/shower valve

### Kitchen Sink
- Garbage disposal: dedicated 20A circuit recommended
- Air gap or high loop required for dishwasher drain (prevents backflow)
- Grease trap: may be required for commercial kitchens

## Backflow Prevention

- Vacuum breaker required on all hose bibs (outdoor faucets)
- Reduced pressure zone (RPZ) assemblies for high-hazard connections
- Air gaps: most effective form of backflow prevention
- Annual testing required for RPZ and double-check assemblies in most jurisdictions
- Irrigation systems: backflow preventer required at point of connection

## Permit and Inspection Notes

Most jurisdictions require permits for:
- Any new plumbing installation (supply or DWV)
- Water heater replacement (yes, even like-for-like)
- Moving or adding fixtures
- Sewer line replacement or repair
- Backflow preventer installation

Common inspection points:
1. **Rough-in inspection:** Before walls are closed — piping layout, proper slope, correct sizing
2. **Top-out inspection:** Vent piping through roof
3. **Final inspection:** Fixtures installed, tested, no leaks

## Common Code Violations

1. S-traps (trap loses its seal via siphoning — must use P-trap with proper vent)
2. Missing T&P discharge pipe on water heater
3. Improper drain slope (too flat = clogs, too steep = solids separation)
4. Trap arm too long (exceeds maximum distance to vent)
5. Double-trapping (two traps in series — creates air lock)
6. Missing cleanouts (required every 100 feet on horizontal runs, at each change of direction >45°)
7. Using wrong pipe material (e.g., DWV fitting on pressure line)
8. Missing shut-off valves at fixtures
9. No expansion tank on closed systems
10. Improper venting (undersized, wrong configuration, or missing entirely)

## State-Specific Notes

- **California:** Title 24 requires low-flow fixtures. Toilets max 1.28 GPF. Showerheads max 1.8 GPM. Many local amendments beyond state code.
- **Florida:** UPC adopted statewide. Polybutylene pipe banned for repairs (replace, don't patch). Hurricane-related code for outdoor fixtures.
- **Texas:** IPC adopted at state level, but local jurisdictions can amend. Some rural areas have no plumbing code enforcement.
- **New York City:** NYC Plumbing Code (based on IPC with significant local amendments). Licensed plumber required for virtually all work.
- **Chicago:** Based on Chicago Building Code — unique requirements not found in UPC or IPC. Cast iron drain pipe required in many applications.

**Always check local AHJ requirements.** The UPC (used in western states) and IPC (used in eastern/central states) have different requirements. Local amendments can add or remove requirements.
